## The one idea

**Every thread of work runs through you.** The human — centered, warm, competent — is the hub. Around them, their agents each work a separate thread, and each one needs the human's judgment *separately*. The human is handing the **same** small golden judgment mark to each agent, one at a time — identical sparks, separate trips. Nothing connects the threads except the person in the middle.

The reader's one-glance feeling: *"that's me — I'm the hub, and it's a lot."* Recognition, not distress. This image shows the problem; the resolutions live later on the page (the bowl in Step 2, the shared constellation in the "+" panel), so this image must not contain them.

## The scene

- **The human** front and center at their desk — the established watercolor person (brown hair, soft sweater). Mid-motion *between* agents: turning from one to the next, a small golden judgment mark leaving their hand toward one agent while another agent waits, attentive. Expression: engaged, capable, the faintest "keeping it all going" — a juggler in control, **not** frazzled, no sweat drops, no chaos.
- **Two or three agent figures** arranged around the human, each at its own small workspace/device — laptop, tablet-with-pen, phone (the established desk-object vocabulary). Each agent is the canonical soft rounded humanoid **but rendered dim** — see glow grammar below. Each looks to the human, friendly and expectant.
- **The judgment marks:** identical small golden star/flower sparks (the `checks-in-motion` vocabulary). One in transit from the human's hand to an agent; perhaps one already delivered, held by a waiting agent; visibly *the same mark, delivered separately* — repetition is the point.
- **Above each agent's work,** a small separate drift of spent sparks thinning out and fading — three little plumes that never meet. No vessel, no bowl, no container anywhere.
- **Light:** time-neutral, warm interior — no window telling the time of day (that's what dated v1). The warmest glow in the room is the judgment marks themselves and the human's presence.

## Glow grammar (new rule this image introduces — get it right)

The agent character's **glow encodes how much of the human's taste and judgment it carries**. In this problem image, the agents are *dimmer* — pale, slightly grey-washed versions of the canonical figure, clearly the same friendly character but unlit (this translates the old grey-robot symbolism — an agent without Soup.net — into the current character). Full warm radiance is reserved for solution imagery later on the page. The judgment marks stay fully golden — the warmth exists, it just has to be hand-carried.
