# Connect Soup.net to Claude

Soup.net is shared memory for taste and judgment. The Claude connector lets Claude **recipe check** Soup.net — retrieving what you've recorded about your preferences and judgments, and logging new ones when you make a call. Every recipe check leaves a trace, so the next one is smarter.

## What the connector adds

Three tools become available to Claude:

- **check_recipe** — Claude makes a recipe check before answering a judgment-call question, then logs the choice you make as a new recipe.
- **get_briefing** — when Claude joins a conversation, it reads the canonical recipe-check guidance plus a sample of your recorded recipes so its suggestions stay aligned with you.
- **list_my_recipe_books** — Claude refreshes that sample mid-conversation if the topic shifts.

You can phrase prompts naturally — Claude handles the structured recipe formulation behind the scenes.

## How to add it

You'll need a Soup.net account — [sign up here](/auth/login).

1. In Claude, open **Settings → Connectors** and click **Add custom connector**.
2. Paste this URL: `https://mcp.soup.net/mcp`
3. Claude will redirect you to Soup.net. Sign in.
4. Choose which recipe books Claude can read and write. You can change this anytime from your Soup.net dashboard.
5. Confirm. The connector is active immediately; Claude will see the new tools on its next reply.

## Example prompts

Three ways people use recipe checks — try one to see the connector working:

- **Broad discovery before starting** — *"I'm drafting my sister's baby shower announcement. Recipe check Soup.net for my taste on writing announcements before you suggest anything."*
- **Judgment between options** — *"I'm picking between Lightroom Cloud and Capture One for my wedding photography. Recipe check Soup.net to see what fits my actual workflow."*
- **Logging a decision** — *"I just settled on raised beds for my tomatoes — the clay soil drains badly. Recipe check that to my garden book."*

## What's shared with Claude

Claude only sees the recipe books you grant access to. Soup.net never sends your email, password, or recipes from books you didn't authorize. You can revoke access at any time from **Claude → Settings → Connectors** or from **Soup.net → Settings → Keys**.

## Privacy

We don't sell your data, we don't advertise, and we don't train AI models on your recipes. Your content is stored in AWS US; vector embeddings are generated by Google Gemini under API-only terms. Full details in the [privacy policy](/info/privacy).
